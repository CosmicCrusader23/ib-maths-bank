<p><em>Marks should be allocated according to the paper 1 markbands for May 2013 forward, part A.</em></p>
<p><em>Answers <strong>may</strong> include</em>:</p>
<ul>
<li>definitions of real GDP, investment, Keynesian multiplier</li>
<li>Keynesian AD/AS diagram to show the multiplier effect (successive increases in AD and real GDP)</li>
<li>explanation of how an initial increase in investment, a component of AD, leads to multiple successive increases in AD and real GDP</li>
<li>hypothetical numerical example(s) to support the explanation or a historical example of specific situations where an increase in investment has possibly led to a multiplier effect.</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Award a maximum of level 2 marks if a candidate does not use the concept of the Keynesian multiplier.</em></p>

<p><em>Answers <strong>may</strong> include</em>:</p>
<ul>

<li>examples of specific interventionist supply-side polices in practice and/or countries where interventionist supply-side policies might have led to economic growth</li>
<li>synthesis or evaluation (discuss).</li>
</ul>
