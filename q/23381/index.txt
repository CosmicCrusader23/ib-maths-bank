<p>positive feedback loops (destabilizing) amplify changes in an ecosystem;<br/>feedback refers to the return of part of the output from a system as input so as to affect succeeding outputs;<br/>this drives the system towards a tipping point where a new equilibrium is adopted;<br/>this new equilibrium may be an alternative stable state / involve collapse of original system;<br/><em>eg</em> increased global temperatures are melting permafrost, leading to the release of methane;<br/>...which is a greenhouse gas and leads to further increases in global temperatures;</p>
<p><em><strong>Note</strong>: Candidates may gain full credit by illustrating the principles of positive feedback by means of a diagram or well-developed example. Credit should be allowed for this</em>.</p>

<p>use of machinery vs human labour, dependency on fossil fuels;<br/>use of organic vs inorganic fertilizers, intensive energy needs of production of inorganic fertilizers/NOx released from use of inorganic fertilizers;<br/>animal vs plant production, animals require more land use due to position in food chain;<br/>types of greenhouse gases produced, <em>eg</em> both rice and animal production produce methane;</p>
<p><em>eg</em> case study: Rice-fish farming in Thailand [1] vs cattle farming in US [1]. Both rice and cattle produce methane, a greenhouse gas [1]. Inorganic fertilisers used in cattle farming releasing nitrogen oxides into atmosphere [1]. Rice is fertilised naturally from fish faeces so has no direct impact on climate change [1]. Cattle farming involves use of heavy machinery / fossil fuels not used in rice fish farming [1]. Rice farming produces food at lower trophic level so absorbs carbon dioxide [1].</p>
