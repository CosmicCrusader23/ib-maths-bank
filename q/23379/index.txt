<p><em>pro-sustainable actions <strong>[4 max]</strong><br/></em>beluga whales have protected status (environmental sustainability) / management has stopped hunting of beluga whales to ensure sustainability in the area;<br/>protected marine areas will ensure nursery areas for continued commercial fishing (environmental/economic sustainability);<br/>protected areas can help to conserve species/biodiversity;<br/>government is working to mitigate damage caused by shipping (reducing ship speed to avoid hitting whales) during migratory season of the right whale (environmental sustainability);<br/>aquaculture development can reduce pressure on wild catch (environmental sustainability);<br/>money/income from oil fields could lead to economic sustainability;<br/>improvements in technology reduces the risk of future spills (environmental sustainability);<br/>whale watching brings in money to local communities (economic sustainability) /activities such as whale watching encourages conservation of species/biodiversity;<br/>commercial fishing can be sustainable with use of quotas/limitations on catch times/changes to net size to reduce bycatch (environmental sustainability);<br/>sewage treatment from cities is in place, reducing nutrient/organic/pathogen loading in water (environmental sustainability);<br/>government has promised $197 million into aquatic research which may lead to increased sustainable management/polices;<br/>government has promised to protect 10 % of Canada’s coastline from fishing and drilling which could protect critical habitats/species.</p>
<p><em>non-sustainable actions</em> <strong><em>[4 max]</em></strong><br/>shipping lanes go through the marine reserve in the St Lawrence River estuary, disturbing beluga/right whales;<br/>shipping brings in invasive species which are changing the ecology of the region;<br/>the protected areas are small and do not correspond with habitat of critically endangered right whales / protected areas do not cover entire range of beluga whales;<br/>beluga numbers have not increased/right whales are being found dead as there is an ongoing problem/management is not effective;<br/>contaminants from aquaculture can cause algal blooms;<br/>species may escape from aquaculture that could adversely affect native species <em>e.g.</em>create competition for resources/reduce genetic diversity/transfer disease to wild populations;<br/>whale watching can interfere/disturb organisms;<br/>release of untreated sewage in the past has adversely affected some species/water uses / there is lack of government management in controlling discharge of untreated sewage / many people do not have access to sewage treatment resulting in the release of untreated wastewater into waterways; <br/>government has proposed sites for oil and gas exploration/drilling which could adversely affect some species/biodiversity;<br/>an oil spill would irreparably harm the organisms within the protected area;<br/>use of non-renewable natural capital/fossil fuels is unsustainable due to their combustion/use resulting in release of carbon dioxide/GHGs/contributing to climate change;<br/>mean temperatures in the Gulf are rising due to global warming;<br/>there is a lack of strategies to deal with the impacts of climate change/global warming on the fishing industry;<br/>no policies in place to manage pollution of POPs/microplastics/heavy metals / release of pollutants such as POPs/heavy metals is harming some species;<br/>rate of commercial fishing which is greater than the growth rate of the fish is not sustainable / depletion of fish stocks by overfishing is not sustainable;<br/>uncontrolled/poorly managed sport fishing could result in the reduction of some species/biodiversity.</p>
