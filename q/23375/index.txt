<p><em>e.g. tundra vs tropical rainforest</em>:</p>
<p><em>Distinguishing features <strong>[3 max]</strong></em>:<br/>tundra has lower insolation / TRF has the highest insolation of all biomes;<br/>TRF has constant insolation throughout the year / tundra has long, dark winters;<br/>tundra has lower mean annual temperature / TRF has warmer temperatures;<br/>tundra has 6–10 months of freezing temperatures / TRF has constant warm temperatures;<br/>TRF has the largest annual precipitation compared to any biome / tundra precipitation is as low as in deserts;<br/>TRF has almost constant precipitation throughout the year;<br/>tundra precipitation mostly in form of snow / tundra has a characteristic layer of frozen ground below the surface/permafrost;<br/>TRFs have the highest biodiversity of all biomes;</p>
<p><em>Explanation of distribution</em>: <em><strong>[2 max]</strong></em><br/>tundra found at the poles, while TRFs at the tropics (above and below the equator);<br/>Hadley cell rises at the Equator causing huge precipitation (low pressure zone) / as warm air cools and its moisture condenses;<br/>tundra is found in the low pressure area of polar cell / at a region where there is net loss of solar energy (causing freezing temperatures) / alpine tundra found on mountaintops, where temperatures are below 0 most time of the year due to high altitude;</p>
<p><em>Notes: Award <strong>[3]</strong> max for correctly indentified distinguishing features i.e. insolation / temperature / precipitation / permafrost / high biodiversity etc</em>.<br/><em>Award <strong>[2] max</strong> for explanation of distribution</em>.<br/><em>Award <strong>[2] max</strong> overall if no biomes are named</em>.</p>
<div class="question_part_label">a.</div>
</div><div class="question" style="padding-left: 20px;">
<p><em>Alternative 1</em>:<br/><em>Method</em>:<br/>light and dark bottle for an aquatic ecosystem;<br/>measure dissolved oxygen at start and end of experiment;<br/>compare measurements in a transparent (with light) and opaque (without light) bottle containing sample of water from ecosystem;<br/>net productivity is equivalent to change in dissolved oxygen in light bottle;<br/>gross productivity is equivalent to change in dissolved oxygen in light bottle plus the loss of dissolved oxygen in the dark bottle (due to respiration);<br/>measurements taken for a set period of time, <em>eg</em> one week;</p>
<p><em>Evaluation</em>:<br/>simple, easy to conduct method;<br/>ethical method – samples can be returned to ecosystem;<br/>difficult to isolate primary producers from consumers in ecosystem sample;<br/>only collecting productivity for submerged subset of ecosystem;<br/>measurements dependent upon temperature;<br/>quality of measurements depends on precision of instruments;</p>
<p><em>Alternative 2</em>:<br/><em>Method</em>:<br/>three comparison plots, one covered in opaque plastic for terrestrial ecosystem;<br/>measure dry biomass at start and end of experiment;<br/>compare measurements from an open (with light) and covered (without light) plot in ecosystem;<br/>net productivity is equivalent to change in biomass in open plot;<br/>gross productivity is equivalent to change in biomass plus the loss of biomass in the covered plot (due to respiration);<br/>measurements taken for a set period of time, <em>eg</em> one week;</p>
<p><em>Evaluation</em>:<br/>easy to isolate primary producers in ecosystem sample;<br/>difficult to collect all biomass;<br/>ethical problems as samples need to be killed to measure dry biomass;<br/>measurements dependent upon temperature;<br/>productivity easier to measure in simpler systems;<br/>difficult to measure with larger producers/trees;</p>
<p><em><strong>Notes</strong>: Award <strong>[3] max</strong> for description of method. Evaluation should address inherent strengths and weaknesses of method. Do not credit examples of poor execution eg inaccurate measurement/samples too small etc</em>.<br/><em>Award <strong>[6] max</strong> if ecosystem is not named</em>.<br/><em>No credit can be given to responses that mistakenly address eg biodiversity rather than productivity</em>.</p>
