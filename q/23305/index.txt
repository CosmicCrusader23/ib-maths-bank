<p>a primary pollutant is one which is active on emission / directly impacts the environment;<br/><em>eg</em> CO<sub>2</sub> is released from burning fossil fuels and actively contributes to global warming / CFCs are released from aerosols and actively contribute to ozone depletion;<br/>a secondary pollutant is one formed from a primary pollutant through physical/chemical change;<br/><em>eg</em> CO<sub>2</sub> combines with sea water to form carbonic acid that leads to impacts on calciferous shelled organisms or corals / NO<sub>x</sub> combines with water to form acid precipitation / NO<sub>2</sub> forms PAN/ozone (that contributes to photochemical smog).</p>
<p><em>Award <strong>[2 max]</strong> if no examples are given.</em><br/><em>Examples of primary pollutants need to include their direct impact (eg NO<sub>x</sub> can be either primary or secondary without such specification) and examples of secondary need to include the process leading to their pollutionary impact).</em><br/><em>Award <strong>[1 max]</strong> for example of primary, and <strong>[1 max]</strong> for example of secondary</em>.</p>
<div class="question_part_label">a.</div>
</div><div class="question" style="padding-left: 20px;">
<p><em>In terrestrial systems <strong>[4 max]</strong></em>:<br/>organic waste such as cattle manure/compost can be added to soil as fertilizer;<br/>its decomposition releases nitrates/phosphates/nutrients that promote plant growth;<br/>the slow release will help to prevent run-off/eutrophication/red tide/algal bloom of water bodies;<br/>improves soil quality/structure / making it less prone to erosion/compaction / increases plowability;<br/>it is similar to the natural organic waste in a terrestrial system so may not be a pollutant.</p>
<p><em>In aquatic systems <strong>[4 max]</strong></em>:<br/>organic waste <em>eg</em> sawdust/domestic sewage/agricultural run-off can be discharged into aquatic systems;<br/>increases turbidity/floats on surface/algal growth reduces light penetration and primary productivity of system;<br/>its decomposition leads to bacterial absorption of O<sub>2</sub> / adds high BOD reducing O<sub>2</sub> availability;<br/>this is a limiting factor in aquatic (not terrestrial) systems (negatively) impacting aquatic life;<br/>(decomposition also) releases nitrates/phosphates/nutrients leading to eutrophication/red tide/algal blooms;<br/>domestic sewage/sawmill effluent may contain non-biodegradable organic pollutants/toxins/pathogens;<br/>can pollute drinking water sources for human populations/animal species.</p>
<p><em>Award <strong>[1]</strong> for each correct explanation, up to <strong>[7 max]</strong></em>.</p>
