The pH of a solution is given by the formula pH=−log10 C where C is the hydrogen ion concentration in a solution, measured in moles per litre (Ml-1).

Find the pH value for a solution in which the hydrogen ion concentration is 5.2×10−8.

[2]
a.

Write an expression for C in terms of pH.

[2]
b.i.

Find the hydrogen ion concentration in a solution with pH 4.2. Give your answer in the form a×10k where 1≤a<10 and k is an integer.

[2]
b.ii.
