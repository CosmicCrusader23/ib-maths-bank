<p><em>Marks should be allocated according to the paper 1 markbands for May 2013 forward, part A. </em></p>
<p><em>Answers <strong>may</strong> include</em>:</p>
<ul>

<li>examples of countries where economic growth has reduced absolute poverty.</li>

<p><em>Marks should be allocated according to the paper 1 markbands for May 2013 forward, part B. </em></p>
<p><em>Answers <strong>may</strong> include</em>:</p>
<ul>
<li>definitions of progressive income tax, equity</li>
<li>diagram to show the impact of progressive income tax on equity</li>
<li>explanation of how progressive income tax increases equity by taxing high-income people at a higher rate, which provides more funds for public services that can increase equity, such as education and healthcare</li>
<li>examples of progressive taxation to increase equity</li>
<li>synthesis or evaluation.</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Evaluation <strong>may</strong> include</em>: consideration of the problems of applying progressive taxation if high-income people avoid paying or people do not expand their businesses and how this can reduce opportunities for people of low incomes.
