<p><em>Refer to the paper 2 assessment criteria when awarding marks</em>. <em>These can be found under the “Your tests” tab &gt; supplemental materials.</em></p>
<p>The command term “discuss” requires candidates to offer a considered review of the use of one or more psychological treatments in relation to one psychological disorder. Psychological treatments employ psychological theory to improve or eliminate symptoms of psychological disorders.</p>
<p>The disorder chosen is likely to come from those presented in the guide:</p>
<ul>
<li>anxiety disorders</li>
<li>depressive disorders</li>
<li>obsessive compulsive disorders</li>
<li>trauma and stress-related disorders</li>
<li>eating disorders.</li>
</ul>
<p>Psychological treatments chosen may include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Interpersonal Therapy (IPT)</li>
<li>Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)</li>
<li>Exposure and Systematic desensitization</li>
<li>Virtual Reality Therapy (VRT)</li>
<li>Mindfulness-based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT)</li>
<li>Group CBT.</li>
</ul>
<p>Descriptions of biological treatments can only be credited if they form part of the critical discussion of psychological ones.</p>
<p>Relevant research may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Mason and Hargreaves’ (2011) qualitative investigation into the effectiveness of MBCT</li>
<li>Butler <em>et al</em>.’s (2006) review of meta-analysis related to CBT efficacy</li>
<li>Hodges and Oei’s (2007) discussion of the applicability of CBT to Chinese culture</li>
<li>McLay <em>et al</em>.’s (2011) assessment of the effectiveness of VRT for post-traumatic stress disorder.</li>
</ul>
<p>Critical discussion in relation to the use of the chosen treatment may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>assumptions and biases</li>
<li>areas of uncertainty</li>
<li>comparison with alternative treatments</li>
<li>link to etiological considerations</li>
<li>advantages and disadvantages of its use</li>
<li>effectiveness based on empirical research</li>
<li>ethical considerations in its use.</li>
</ul>
<p>If a candidate discusses treatment for more than one psychological disorder, credit should be given only to the part of the response related to the first psychological disorder.</p>
