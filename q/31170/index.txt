<p>Merv was also no stranger to political upheaval and war … [but no] conquest was as traumatic as its pillage by the Mongols in 1221 … According to the [Muslim] historian Ibn al-Athir, who based his account on the reports of refugees from Merv: “Genghis Khan ordered the troops who had been seized should be brought before him … they were executed and the people looked on and wept. They said, ‘These people have resisted us’ so they killed them all.”</p>
