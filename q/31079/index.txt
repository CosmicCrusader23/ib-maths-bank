<p><em>Refer to the paper 1 section B assessment criteria when awarding marks</em>.</p>
<p>The command term “discuss” requires candidates to offer a considered and balanced review of one or more ethical considerations related to one or more research studies at the cognitive level of analysis.</p>
<p>Ethical considerations may be positive (what guidelines were followed) or negative (what guidelines were not followed).</p>
<p>Ethical considerations may include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>deception</li>
<li>protection from physical or mental harm</li>
<li>briefing and debriefing</li>
<li>right to withdraw from a study</li>
<li>informed consent</li>
<li>anonymity/confidentiality.</li>
</ul>
<p>Discussion of ethical considerations may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>why deception is used</li>
<li>the need for debriefing</li>
<li>a cost–benefit analysis approach with regard to ethical considerations</li>
<li>the meaning of “informed” consent and who has the right to give it</li>
<li>changes over time in adherence to ethical standards/guidelines</li>
<li>the importance of minimizing physical and/or psychological pain</li>
<li>why anonymity/confidentiality of data is important</li>
<li>whether the study is justified or could it have been conducted in alternative ways.</li>
</ul>
<p>Candidates that discuss the use of animals in research should use studies that are clearly cognitive and not studies that focus on the effects of biological factors on cognitive processes.</p>
