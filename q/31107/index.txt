<p><em>Refer to the paper 1 section A markbands when awarding marks</em>. <em>These can be found under the “Your tests” tab &gt; supplemental materials.</em></p>
<p>The command term “explain” requires candidates to give a detailed account, including reasons or causes, of one cultural dimension.</p>
<p>Cultural dimensions may include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>individualism versus collectivism (Berry, 1967; Chen <em>et al</em>., 2005)</li>
<li>power/distance (Zhang <em>et al</em>., 2010; Lynn <em>et al</em>., 1993; Eylon and Au, 1999)</li>
<li>long-term versus short-term orientation (Confucian dynamism) (Hofstede and Bond, 1988)</li>
<li>masculinity versus femininity (Vunderick and Hofstede, 1998)</li>
<li>uncertainty avoidance (Shane, 1995).</li>
</ul>
<p>Responses should present the core traits that define the cultural dimension. For example, individualistic societies focus on uniqueness, achievement and freedom, whereas collectivistic societies focus on family, relationships and a common fate or heritage.</p>
<p>Candidates could explain the chosen cultural dimension generally, such as it is the effect of a culture on the beliefs and values of a society, or in a more detailed manner with explanations based on social mobility, agricultural versus urban, democratic principles, economic stability, <em>etc</em>. Both explanations are equally acceptable.</p>
<p>If a candidate explains more than one cultural dimension, credit should be given only to the first explanation.</p>
<p>If a candidate describes a relevant study, but a cultural dimension is not explained, up to a maximum of <strong>[4]</strong> should be awarded.</p>
<p>If a candidate explains one cultural dimension without reference to a study, up to a maximum of <strong>[6]</strong> should be awarded. </p>
