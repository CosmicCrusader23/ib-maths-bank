<p><em>Refer to the paper 2 assessment criteria when awarding marks. </em></p>
<p>The command term “analyse” requires candidates to bring out (emphasize) the essential aspects of cultural variation in gender roles.</p>
<p>Responses may address how sociocultural factors such as media, stereotypes, ethnic and cultural experiences, peer, school and parental roles influence gender roles. It is appropriate to address cross-cultural differences in gender roles related to behaviour such as aggression, workplace roles/status, parenting behaviour, domestic work, and so on.</p>
<p>Relevant research may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Eagly’s social role theory</li>
<li>Bandura’s social learning theory</li>
<li>gender schema theory</li>
<li>Money’s theory on gender roles</li>
<li>Mead’s (1935) anthropological study</li>
<li>Best <em>et al.</em>’s (1977) cross-cultural study on gender stereotypes</li>
<li>Cuddy <em>et al.</em>’s (2010) study on how gender stereotypes are shaped in different cultures with participants attributing positive traits to men</li>
<li>Smith and Lloyd’s (1978) experiment on the perception of gender.</li>
</ul>
<p>Examples of how candidates may show evidence of critical thinking may include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>evidence from studies concerning the existence of cultural variation in gender roles</li>
<li>the methodological and/or ethical considerations of research</li>
<li>whether sociocultural influences create gender differences or merely accentuate them</li>
<li>whether differences between males and females are purely social constructs or a result of biological differences</li>
<li>differences between collectivistic versus individualistic societies</li>
<li>interaction between biological, cognitive and cultural factors.</li>
</ul>
<p>Candidates may present one analysis of cultural variation in gender roles in order to demonstrate depth of knowledge, or may present a number of analyses of cultural variation in gender roles in order to demonstrate breadth of knowledge. Both approaches are equally acceptable.</p>
