<p>biodiversity includes the diversity of species, habitat and genes;<br/>species diversity involves both the variety/number of species/richness and their relative proportions/evenness;<br/>habitat diversity refers to the range of different habitats in an ecosystem or biome;<br/>...which may vary due to environmental gradients/changing abiotic conditions/altitude/latitude/major disturbances (volcanic activity/landslides, <em>etc</em>);<br/>habitat/niche diversification promotes species diversity;<br/>genetic diversity refers to the range of genetic material/genes in a population/species;<br/>...which is influenced by mutation/sexual reproduction/natural selection/speciation;<br/>high primary productivity/insolation/precipitation/optimum abiotic conditions promote biodiversity;<br/>succession promotes greater biodiversity by increasing length/branching of food chains / because it leads to improved abiotic conditions.<em><br/></em></p>
<p><em>Credit can be gained through description of particular ecosystems eg. climax communities; hotspots; but the specific factors must be identified to gain credit.<br/>Do not credit human impacts or any factor reducing biodiversity.</em></p>
<p> </p>
<div class="question_part_label">a.</div>
</div><div class="question" style="padding-left: 20px;">
<p>named ecosystem, <em>eg</em> shallow stream running through woodland;</p>
<p>studies should be made before and after human activity/in proximal and distal positions from activity;<br/><em>Explanation</em>: to provide evidence of human causation/correlation;</p>
<p>transects and quadrats may be used to sample area;<br/><em>Exp</em>: to reduce overall workload but be representative of area;</p>
<p>sampling should be repeated over time/distance;<br/><em>Exp</em>: to increase reliability;</p>
<p>abundance/biomass of biotic components may be measured;<br/><em>Exp</em>: to assess whether population size/productivity of populations/species have been affected.</p>
<p>numbers of motile organisms can be measured using Lincoln Index/mark–release–recapture;<br/><em>Exp</em>: because quadrats are ineffective if organisms are constantly on the move;</p>
<p>abiotic components may be measured using probes/thermometers/chemical tests, <em>etc</em>;<br/><em>Exp</em>: because human activity may alter prevailing abiotic conditions;</p>
<p>indicator species may be identified/quantified (using keys);<br/><em>Exp</em>: because their presence/abundance will indicate degree of human impact;</p>
<p>species diversity can be measured using a diversity index (<em>eg</em> Simpson’s); <br/><em>Exp</em>: because the index takes into account both species richness and equitability/distribution/relative abundance / can quantitatively compare different habitats/same habitat over time;</p>
<p>genetic &amp; habitat diversity can be identified;<br/><em>Exp</em>: because these are also components of overall biodiversity.</p>
<p><em>Award <strong>[6 max]</strong> if no named ecosystem.</em><br/><em>Award <strong>[4 max]</strong> if no explanations for use of techniques are given</em><br/><em>There should be no credit given for extra detail of methods/techniques or for potential results of those methods. Focus of question is on purpose of techniques</em>.</p>
