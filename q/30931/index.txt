<p><em>Refer to the paper 1 section B assessment criteria when awarding marks</em>. <em>These can be found under the “Your tests” tab &gt; supplemental materials.</em> </p>
<p>The command term “discuss” requires candidates to offer a considered review of how one or more hormones and/or one or more pheromones affect behaviour.</p>
<p>The effect of hormones/pheromones may be discussed in relation to any type of behaviour such as, but not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>attraction</li>
<li>aggression</li>
<li>trust</li>
<li>emotional response</li>
<li>attachment.</li>
</ul>
<p>Relevant studies may include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Lundstrom and Olsson’s (2005) study on effects of androstadienone on women's attraction to men</li>
<li>Wedekind et al. (1995) investigating mate preference based on genetic makeup in relation to immune system functioning</li>
<li>Doucet's <em>et al.</em>’s (2009) study on maternal behaviour</li>
<li>Saxton <em>et al.</em>’s (2008) study on how androstadienone modulates women's attributions of men's attractiveness.</li>
<li>Radke <em>et al.</em>’s (2015) study investigating the effects of testosterone on women’s responses to angry faces</li>
<li>Wagels <em>et al.</em>’s (2017) study showing that testosterone reduced desired personal distance from aggressive humans</li>
<li>Albert <em>et al.</em>’s (1986) Testosterone and aggression in mice</li>
<li>Baumgartner <em>et al.</em>’s (2008) study on the role of oxytocin on trust in economic behaviour</li>
<li>McGaugh and Cahill’s study on adrenaline and flashbulb memory</li>
</ul>
<p>Critical discussion may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>methodological and/or ethical considerations related to research investigating how hormones and/or pheromones affect human behaviour including the ambiguity of research evidence</li>
<li>to what extent pheromones have an effect on human behaviour</li>
<li>how the findings of the research have been interpreted and applied</li>
<li>implications of the findings</li>
<li>supporting and/or contradictory evidence</li>
<li>the value of animal research.</li>
</ul>
<p>Animal research may be used to describe the effect of pheromones and hormones, but the response must then be linked to human behaviour. If there is no explicit link to human behaviour the response should be awarded up to a maximum of <strong>[3]</strong> for criterion D.</p>
