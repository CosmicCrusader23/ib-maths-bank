<p>the value and status of natural capital may vary regionally;<br/><em>eg</em> cork may be highly valued in areas where grapes for wine are grown;<br/>the value may also vary over time;<br/><em>eg</em> cork has been used for millennia as bottle stoppers for wine and other products but in the last 20 years its value has decreased as other resources have been used to seal wine bottles;<br/>the value may vary due to other reasons, <em>eg</em> social/political;<br/><em>eg</em> uranium’s value decreased quickly after the Fukushima nuclear disaster when public pressure led to several countries declaring they would phase out nuclear power;<br/>the value may vary due to environmental/technological reasons;<br/><em>eg</em> lithium’s value has increased as it is used to make batteries for electric cars and personal devices;</p>
<p><em>Award <strong>[1]</strong> for each correct reason and/or example, up to <strong>[4 max]</strong>. N.B. Credit may be allowed for alternative egs of equivalent validity, detail and relevance</em>.</p>
<div class="question_part_label">a.</div>
</div><div class="question" style="padding-left: 20px;">
<p>natural resources like water/food/productive land/fossil fuel/ore deposits are distributed unequally around the globe/some countries have a lot, some others have few;<br/><em>eg</em> sub-Saharan African countries face water shortages/Middle East countries have a huge surplus of oil;<br/>which may lead one country to invade another for its resources <em>eg</em> ore deposits in Congo/Afghanistan;<br/>inequitabilities may also arise from changes within societies due to over consumption/population growth/lack of technology (agricultural/extraction/mining)/unsustainable development;<br/>…or from changes in their surroundings <em>eg</em> climatic change/international politics/economics/war/embargoes;<br/><em>eg</em> food price crisis in 2008 causing protests/riots/political/economic/social unrest (in both LEDCs &amp; MEDCs);<br/><em>eg</em> water scarcity due to climate change in Syria is argued to be a major cause of civil war in 2011;<br/>Inequitability often leads to conflict when a resource is shared across national borders (usually water storages or oil deposits);<br/><em>eg</em> sharing the Nile’s water resources between Ethiopia, Sudan and Egypt;<br/>inequitability in energy/fuel reserves is particularly significant to economics/national security;<br/><em>eg</em> leading to, and promulgating many conflicts in the Middle East;<br/>conflicts can also occur between constituencies within a country <em>eg</em> social classes/ethnic groups/resentments over government regulations/bans/taxation/private ownerships v public;<br/><em>eg</em> when cost of clean water is prohibitive for lower social classes;</p>
<p><em>Award 1 mark for any argument/valid eg connecting unequal resource distribution to conflict;</em><br/><em>Allow 4 max for outlining inequality in resource distribution without clear reference on how it is leading to conflict</em>.</p>
