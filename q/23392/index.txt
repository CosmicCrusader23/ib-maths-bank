<p><em>DDT</em>…</p>
<ol style="list-style-type:lower-roman;">
<li>is a very effective/affordable insecticide;</li>
<li>can help in the management of insect borne diseases/malaria/zika;</li>
<li>can help in the control of agricultural pests, improving harvests;</li>
<li>is a persistent (organic) pollutant / causes soil degradation/aquatic pollution;</li>
<li>has negative effects on human health;</li>
<li>bioaccumulates in the bodies of organisms;</li>
<li>biomagnifies in food chains;</li>
<li>causes impact on non-target species/death of birds of prey/top carnivores;</li>
<li>reduces biodiversity;</li>
</ol>
<div class="question_part_label">a.</div>
</div><div class="question" style="padding-left: 20px;">
<p><em>Introductory explanations</em>:</p>
<ol style="list-style-type:lower-roman;">
<li>ozone depleting substances (ODSs)/CFCs disrupt (the dynamic equilibrium of) ozone formation;</li>
<li>ODSs are halogenated organic gases such as chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) / halons;</li>
<li>....used as cooling agents (old refrigerators, air-conditioning units) / aerosol propellants (spray cans) / blowing agents (insulating foams) / fire-extinguishers / cleaning solvents (electronic equipment) / pesticides;<br/><br/><em>Activities that lead to increase/restoration of ozone</em>:</li>
<li>pollution management may be achieved by reducing the manufacture and release of ozone-depleting substances;</li>
<li>methods for this reduction include recycling refrigerants/developing alternatives to gas-blown plastics/ halogenated pesticides/propellants and aerosols/developing non-propellant alternatives;</li>
<li>the Montreal Protocol (on Substances that Deplete the Ozone Layer (1987)) (and subsequent updates) is an international agreement for the reduction of use of ODSs;</li>
<li>this is considered to be a very successful international agreement;</li>
<li>national governments complying with the agreement made national laws and regulations to decrease the consumption/production of ODSs;<br/><br/><em>Mechanisms that lead to reduction/destruction of ozone</em>:</li>
<li>UV light breaks apart the ODSs/CFCs and releases a halogen atom;</li>
<li>halogen atom breaks an ozone molecule apart and combines with an oxygen atom;</li>
<li>oxygen atoms combine with the oxygen from the halogen and release the halogen to start the process again;</li>
<li>.… the halogen is considered a catalyst / why the reaction is cyclical;<br/><br/><em>Explanations of “continuing” effect on ozone</em>:</li>
<li>ODSs/CFCs have a very long lifetime in the atmosphere / there is a time lag from removal of ODSs to the end of ozone destruction;</li>
<li>halogen atoms are removed very slowly from stratosphere; (<em>Cl by a chemical reaction with methane forming hydrochloric acid</em>)</li>
<li>an illegal market for ozone-depleting substances persists and requires consistent monitoring;</li>
<li>some countries are still not complying with international agreements on bans;</li>
<li>HCFCs may have lower ozone depleting potential, but still destroy ozone (not phased out before 2020);</li>
<li>increasing GCC/GHGs affect polar weather conditions (increasing temperature difference between troposphere and cooler stratosphere), thus affecting the distribution of ozone (positively in 2019, negatively in 2020); (<em><strong>Note</strong> that this should be the only link with GCC accepted</em>)</li>
</ol>
<p><em><strong>Notes</strong>: Award <strong>[5 max]</strong> if there are no explanations of “continuing” effect on ozone.</em><br/><em>Accept CFCs as alternative to ODSs</em>.</p>
