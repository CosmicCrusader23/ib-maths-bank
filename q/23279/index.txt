<p><em>Argument for development away from traditional lifestyles</em> <em><strong>[4 max]</strong></em>:<br/>reduction in tavy agriculture would reduce deforestation/carbon dioxide emissions/soil erosion/soil degradation / tavy is unsustainable because it results in loss of trees/carbon dioxide emissions/soil erosion/soil degradation / increase in alternative practices <em>e.g.</em> agroforestry could increase sustainability by reducing soil degradation/reducing soil erosion/increasing carbon sink;<br/>increase in tourism services could increase investment in conservation / establishing more national parks (to increase tourism) would limit deforestation/protect wildlife;<br/>increase in service industry could reduce dependency on agriculture <em>e.g.</em> tavy;<br/>increase education opportunities lead to better environmental awareness;<br/>increased use of family planning/smaller family sizes could reduce population growth and potentially in the long term lower Madagascar’s ecological footprint;<br/>migration from rural areas will reduce impact in these areas;<br/>traditional beliefs that aye-aye are evil/bring bad luck/pests and should be killed could negatively impact their population/increase their risk of extinction;<br/>alternative energy sources such as solar/wind power could be more sustainable than the traditional use of charcoal / use of renewable energy does not deplete resources (<em>e.g.</em> wood/fossil fuels)/produces lower emissions of carbon dioxide/greenhouse gases.</p>
<p><em>Against <strong>[4 max]</strong></em>:<br/>use of alternative farming methods such as intensive farming could lead to soil degradation/loss of nutrients from soil/increase in runoff containing pesticides/nutrients damaging habitats;<br/>migration to/growth of urban/tourist centres could increase deforestation to build more housing;<br/>development of tourist resort/urbanisation could increase loss of mangrove forest/increase edge effect;<br/>migration to/growth of urban/tourist centres could increase water stress in those areas;<br/>migration to/growth of urban/tourist centres could increase discharges of sewage effluent that have a negative impact in those areas / sewage waste from tourism could damage reef systems/cause eutrophication within aquatic systems;<br/>urbanization/growth of tourist centres could reduce biocapacity/amount of productiveland;<br/>increase in tourism/urbanisation could lead to more roads that damage/fragment habitats;<br/>tourism could increase damage to coral reefs through boat anchors/trampling;<br/>country does not have systems in place to manage higher levels of solid domestic waste production from tourism / increased littering/waste disposal from tourism could damage habitats/species;<br/>ecological footprint has stayed relatively stable (since 1961);<br/>change in lifestyle is likely to increase consumerism/ecological footprint;<br/>it would take time for land/forest that has been degraded (<em>e.g.</em> through tavy) to become suitable for agroforestry;<br/>food production may become less localized/more intensive;<br/>fewer farmers could lead to dependency on importation of food.</p>
<p><em>Award <strong>[5 max]</strong> for arguments for and against</em>.</p>
<p><em>Conclusion <strong>[1 mark]</strong></em> <em>e.g.</em>:<br/>change to agroforestry is likely to increase sustainability but it is not so clear that ecotourism will.</p>
