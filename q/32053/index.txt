<p>Candidates are required to offer a considered and balanced review of the impact of neocolonialism on two countries in the region in the given period. Neocolonialism suggests that while countries achieved nominal political independence, their economies continued to follow models of colonialism. Thus, economic development was based on primary export economies and remained dependent on conditions and policies of countries outside the region.</p>
<p><em>Indicative content</em></p>
<p><strong>Economic influence</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>It contributed to an export boom in, for example, sugar, coffee and minerals, and it led to the development of cash crop economies in many countries.</li>
<li>It promoted foreign investment in infrastructure, for example, in railroads and communications.</li>
<li>In some areas estate values soared, which benefitted large landowners.</li>
<li>Foreign companies, such as fruit companies, established themselves in many countries, and even low managerial levels were occupied by foreigners.</li>
<li>Industrial development was still limited.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Political influence</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>An amount of political stability was achieved, but managed elections and fraud prevailed.</li>
<li>In some countries, Mexico, Chile and Argentina for example, the urban middle class began to demand greater political participation.</li>
<li>The US increased its influence in the region intervening in Cuba, Panama, Mexico and several other Central American and Caribbean countries.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Social influence</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>There was internal rural migration and foreign migrations to cities.</li>
<li>Bureaucracy grew as did the urban middle class.</li>
<li>Foreign cultural influence could be seen in areas such as education, arts and fashion.</li>
<li>It could be argued that neocolonialism reinforced social stratification.</li>
<li>Indigenous populations and peasants saw limited improvement in their lives. Many became landless peasants as a result of the economic policies.</li>
</ul>
