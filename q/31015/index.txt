<p><em>Refer to the paper 2 markbands when awarding marks.</em></p>
<p>The command term “discuss” requires candidates to offer a considered review of the factors related to overeating and the development of obesity.</p>
<p>It is not necessary for candidates to make a distinction between overeating and obesity.</p>
<p>Factors may include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>physiological factors – for example, genetic predisposition, the role of dopamine, neurobiological explanation of food addiction</li>
<li>psychological/cognitive factors – for example, low self-esteem, distorted body image, pessimistic thinking patterns, cognitive restraint</li>
<li>sociocultural factors – for example, sedentary lifestyle, high-fat diet, coping with poverty.</li>
</ul>
<p>Relevant research may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Stunkard et al.’s (1990) study of identical twins reared apart</li>
<li>theory of compulsive overeating – food craving is related to secretion of dopamine in the brain’s reward circuit</li>
<li>Volkow et al.’s (2002) fMRI (functional magnetic resonance imaging) study indicating that obese participants had the same deficiency in dopamine receptors as drug addicts</li>
<li>restraint theory – due to either external triggers or emotional experiences a person is more likely to experience a lack of control that leads to overeating</li>
<li>Jeffery (2001): an increasingly sedentary way of life leads to more people suffering from the results of obesity.</li>
</ul>
<p>Discussion may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>cultural and ethical considerations</li>
<li>empirical evidence and related methodological factors</li>
<li>interaction between biological, cognitive, and sociocultural factors.</li>
</ul>
<p>Candidates may discuss two factors related to overeating and the development of obesity in order to demonstrate depth of knowledge, or may discuss a larger number of factors related to overeating and the development of obesity in order to demonstrate breadth of knowledge. Both approaches are equally acceptable.</p>
<p>If a candidate discusses only one factor, the response should be awarded up to a maximum of [5] for criterion A, knowledge and comprehension, up to a maximum of [4] for criterion B, critical thinking, and up to a maximum of [2] for criterion C, organization.</p>
