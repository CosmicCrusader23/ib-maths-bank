<ol style="list-style-type:lower-roman;">
<li>albedo is a measure of the reflectiveness of a surface / is greater from lighter-coloured/smoother surfaces;</li>
<li>high albedo means that solar radiation is reflected away from a surface/reducing temperature / low albedo means that solar radiation is absorbed by a surface/increasing temperature;</li>
<li>oceans/ice/clouds contribute significantly to Earth’s albedo;</li>
<li>the balance between the albedo of the Earth’s surfaces involves feedback loops;</li>
<li>negative feedback loops reduce change (retain the balance in ratio of albedo) and maintain the global temperature;</li>
<li>e.g. rise of global temperature <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> increased evaporation <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> increased cloud cover <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> increased albedo and reflection of solar radiation <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> decrease in global temperature;</li>
<li>a change in the balance can result in a positive feedback loop which amplifies changes and results in a rise in the Earth’s global temperature;</li>
<li>e.g. rise in global temperature <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> increase melting in ice caps <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> decrease in albedo <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> increase in solar radiation absorption <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><mo>→</mo></math> rise in global temperature;</li>
</ol>
