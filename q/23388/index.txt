<p><em>Altering human activity</em>:<br/>changing transportation use to reduce private transportation / increase public transportation / walking/biking;<br/>this can be expensive due to infrastructure improvements required;<br/>requires public buy-in / change in behaviour;<br/>successful if a reduction in use of fossil fuels for transportation needs;<br/>successful if good network of charging stations / footpaths/bike lanes to encourage change;<br/>addresses root cause of pollution/prevents any damage in first place;</p>
<p>education of public regarding value of renewable energies/impacts of pollution;<br/>this can influence more environmentally friendly choices;<br/>influences attitudes/values in future generations;<br/>but maybe the local governments/authorities that need influencing more than general public;</p>
<p><em>Controlling release of pollutant</em>:<br/>reduce sulphur content of fossil fuels using trading system or pollution budgets;<br/>has proved very successful in USA where government put legislation in place to support this;<br/>increases costs as low sulphur fuels are more expensive / requires technological investment;</p>
<p>use catalytic converters on car exhausts/scrubbers/CATS;<br/>very effective at reducing nitrous oxides;<br/>...but expensive;<br/>use heavy metals which need to be mined; <br/>catalysts need replacing frequently;<br/>effective but requires investment in the technology and expensive;</p>

<p>increase efficiency of power production and demand;<br/>advantage that it reduces all pollutants associated with power production;</p>
<p>taxation systems and trading mechanisms;<br/>very effective when implemented with political support;<br/>requires improvement in public transportation infrastructure;<br/>could be argued that not ethical as encourages trading of pollutants;</p>
<p>international legislation to control emissions (<em>eg</em> Sulphur Emissions Reduction Protocol and the Convention on Long Range Transboundary Air Pollution);<br/>have been successful in reducing sulphur dioxide emissions;<br/>...but led to increase in nitrous oxide production;<br/>can be difficult to implement and monitor;</p>
<p><em>Clean-up and restoration of damaged systems</em>:<br/>adding limestone powder to acidified lakes;<br/>effective and cheap solution;<br/>but does not remove the cause of the problem;<br/>environmental impact of mining the limestone;</p>
<p>restocking lakes after remediation;<br/>needs careful management and unlikely to return the lake to the prior system;</p>
<p>reafforestation/replanting of damaged forests;<br/>expensive;<br/>takes long time for restoration;<br/>new growth acts as effect carbon sink;</p>
