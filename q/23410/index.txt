<p>natural capital refers to natural resources that can supply a natural income of goods or services;<br/>natural income is the yield/growth obtained from natural resources/capital;<br/>to be sustainable, natural capital must be used at rate slower than its replenishment / within maximum sustainable yield / that fulfils current needs without compromising future availability;<br/>if more than the natural income is extracted, then the use is unsustainable;<br/>exploitation of natural capital may also be unsustainable due to processes of extraction/transport/processing / environment should be in same condition as at outset to be sustainable;<br/>altering human behaviour/values/choices through policies/legislations/campaigns so that unsustainable exploitation of natural capital is reduced;<br/>non-renewable natural capital is either irreplaceable or can only be replaced over geological timescales / non-renewable natural capital can never be used sustainably.</p>
<p><em>The question addresses the essential nature (concept) of sustainability rather than specific practices. However, do credit candidates for valid and specific examples if they exemplify any of the generic principles above</em>.</p>
<div class="question_part_label">a.</div>
</div><div class="question" style="padding-left: 20px;">
<p>environmental indicators (EIs) may involve measures of biodiversity/<br/>pollution/population/climate/emissions/resource consumption;<br/>EIs are tools for measuring progress toward sustainability/supporting policy evaluation/informing the public/comparing nations;<br/>EIs involve the setting of measurable goals from established baseline measurements;<br/>EIs can be used at a range of scales from local to global;<br/>after some time, measures can be reassessed and compared to the baselines/domestic objectives/international agreements (<em>eg</em> Kyoto/Montreal Protocol);<br/>environmental impact assessments (EIAs) are EIs that measure a wide profile of indicators usually before and after some development;<br/>ecological footprints(EFs) are EIs that focus on rates of consumption compared to rates of natural income;<br/>if a population’s EF is greater than the area available this indicates unsustainability;<br/>the Millennium Ecosystem Assessment used indicators to give a scientific appraisal of the condition/trends in the world’s ecosystems and services;<br/>certain species can be used as indicators of pollution;<br/>some indicator species are particularly sensitive to pollution so a low abundance may suggest heavy pollution / high abundance suggest clean environment (<em>eg </em>lichens);<br/>loss of these sensitive species may be an effective indicator for appealing to the public;<br/>some indicator species are particularly resistant to pollution so a high abundance/dominance of these species may suggest high pollution (<em>eg</em> tubifex worms/coliform bacteria).</p>
