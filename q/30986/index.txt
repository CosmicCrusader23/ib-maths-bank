<p><em>Refer to the paper 2 assessment criteria when awarding marks. </em></p>
<p>The command term “evaluate” requires candidates to make an appraisal of one or more treatments for obesity by weighing up the strengths and the limitations of each. Although a discussion of both strengths and limitations is required, it does not have to be evenly balanced to gain high marks.</p>
<p>Treatment choices include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>the behavioural programme developed by Stuart and Davis (1972) for a clinical setting</li>
<li>cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) combined with dieting (<em>eg </em>Beck, 2005)</li>
<li>the psycho-social method proposed by Blair-West (2007) that combines goal setting, diet, physical activity and cognitive awareness</li>
<li>drug treatments such as appetite suppressants and lipase inhibitors</li>
<li>surgical procedures such as gastric bypass and gastric banding</li>
<li>treatments such as Weight Watchers.</li>
</ul>
<p>Evaluation of the treatments may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>cultural and gender considerations</li>
<li>empirical findings</li>
<li>ethical considerations</li>
<li>conditions where the treatments may be employed</li>
<li>comparison to other treatment methods.</li>
</ul>
<p>Candidates may address one treatment in order to demonstrate depth of knowledge or may address a larger number of treatments in order to demonstrate breadth of knowledge. Both approaches are equally acceptable.</p>
<p>If a candidate addresses only strengths or only limitations, the response should be awarded up to a maximum of <strong>[5] </strong>for critical thinking and up to a maximum of <strong>[2] </strong>for organization. Up to full marks may be awarded for knowledge and comprehension.</p>
<p>Responses that only address prevention strategies should be awarded up to a maximum of <strong>[3] </strong>for criterion A, knowledge and comprehension, up to a maximum of <strong>[3] </strong>for criterion B, critical thinking, and up to a maximum of <strong>[2] </strong>for criterion C, organization.</p>
