<p>atmospheric/tri-cellular circulation (including Hadley, Ferrel &amp; polar cells) creates patterns of climate that determine dominant vegetation types;<br/>low pressure due to intense heating/high insolation at the equator causes / rising moist air in the tropics creates high precipitation giving rise to rainforests;<br/>moving polewards (at high altitude), air cools, becomes denser and sinks forming a high-pressure zone / descending/dry air (20–30° latitude/tropics) creates water-limiting/arid conditions giving rise to deserts;<br/>some of the air continues towards the poles to equalize temperature difference / atmosphere transfers heat from (sub-)tropics to mid-latitudes giving rise to temperate biomes;<br/>descending/dry air (high latitude/polar regions) creates water-limiting conditions in tundra;<br/>water vapour (from mid-latitudes/temperate regions) is transferred to high latitudes giving rise to heavy precipitation/snow in boreal forest;<br/>water vapour is transferred from ocean surfaces overland generating freshwater aquatic systems;<br/>prevailing winds/jet streams (blowing from high to low pressure) bring precipitation to a region, e.g. temperate rainforest in mountainous region/riverine/water-margin systems;<br/>rain shadow effect of high mountains causes dry winds in the leeward side, resulting in arid or semi-arid biomes (e.g. Tibetan Plateau, Mongolian Gobi desert and steppes);<br/>atmosphere may be responsible for shifting biomes due to global warming/climate change;</p>
