<li>limiting geographical/climatic factors;</li>

<p><em>The following examples of water management strategies and their evaluation show how credit can be given for any appropriate strategy</em>.</p>
<p><em>Example 1</em>: Rainwater harvesting;<br/><em>Positive</em>:<br/>does not impact natural water cycle / replenishment rate / impossible to reduce natural income;<br/>free natural capital;<br/>useful for watering plants/irrigation / washing / fire protection / (thus) reduces consumption of groundwater/other freshwater resources;</p>
<p><em>Negative</em>:<br/>availability restricted temporally and spatially (unequal distribution of rain / unpredictable supply);<br/>usually not safe for drinking;<br/>can’t satisfy needs of irrigation of commercial agriculture;</p>
<p><em>Example 2</em>: Desalinization;<br/><em>Positive</em>:<br/>sustainable if energy required produced by photovoltaic cells;<br/>sea water is more available than freshwater;<br/>provides accessible/safe drinking/irrigation water;<br/>salt may be used for producing useful chemical products (sodium hydroxide, hydrochloric acid);<br/>reduces pressure on freshwater reserves that need protection;</p>
<p><em>Negative</em>:<br/>requires huge amounts of energy / increase GCC if fossil fuel used;<br/>not available in landlocked countries;<br/>building of facilities result in environmental damage/pollution;<br/>disposing of salt (brine) poses environmental hazards/pollutes ocean / salt is contaminated so can’t be eaten;<br/>high cost to build and operate that may be economically unsustainable;</p>
<p><em><strong>Notes</strong>: Award <strong>[1 mark]</strong> for each appropriately identified strategy for improving access to freshwater up to <strong>[2 max]</strong></em>.<br/><em>Do not accept simplistic statements not clearly linked to sustainability, e.g. cheap / efficient / doesn’t need elaborate equipment/scientific expertise.</em><br/><em>Accept statements such as “its cost is too great to be economically sustainable / its cost may divert economic resources away from habitat/species protection / resource requirement maybe unsustainable for LEDCs”.</em><br/><em>Award <strong>[5 max]</strong> if only positive OR only negative evaluation OR only one strategy evaluated.</em><br/><em>Award <strong>[1 max]</strong> for each clear negative or positive evaluation of each factor</em>.</p>
