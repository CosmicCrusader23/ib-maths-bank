<p>The command term “discuss” requires candidates to offer a considered review of ethical considerations in relation to the treatment of psychological disorders.</p>
<p>Relevant ethical considerations can include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>effectiveness of treatments</li>
<li>informed consent</li>
<li>validity of diagnosis</li>
<li>physical and psychological harm caused by treatment</li>
<li>the expectations of those who are treated and/or worsening of symptoms</li>
<li>short and long-term side effects of treatments</li>
<li>the responsibilities of the therapist</li>
<li>cultural/gender sensitivity.</li>
</ul>
<p>Research that can be used to support the discussion may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Rossello and Bernal’s (1999) study adapting CBT and IPT for Puerto Rican adolescents</li>
<li>Aultman’s (2016) case study on psychiatric diagnostic uncertainty in patient-centred care</li>
<li>Hoop et al.’s (2008) study on ethics and culture in mental healthcare</li>
<li>Garnets et al.’s (1991) study on issues in psychotherapy in lesbians and gay men.</li>
</ul>
<p>Critical discussion may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>the considerations of conducting treatment in a different culture</li>
<li>the use of blind and double-blind research when investigating the effectiveness of treatment, including the use of placebo</li>
<li>the difficulties of ensuring confidentiality and anonymity</li>
<li>decisions as to why certain ethical guidelines were/were not followed in research investigating the effectiveness of treatment</li>
<li>limitations of research investigating effectiveness of specific treatments e.g. the issue of long term effectiveness</li>
<li>social sensitivity of studies investigating the treatment of psychological disorders.</li>
</ul>
